Transportation

Metro

The metro is underground transportation that travels through the city.




Reasons to Use the Metro



  • The metro is fast, inexpensive to use, and runs frequently.
  • Maps for the metro are easy to follow.
  • Signs for route transfers in the tunnels are well marked.


Tips for Using the Metro

  • One t+ ticket is good for the following forms of transportation: the metro, RER Zone 1, regional buses, trams, or the Montmarte Funicular.
  • One ticket permits unlimited transfers for 90 minutes to any metro line or the RER in Zone 1 (central Paris).
  • Another option for the tickets permits transfers between buses and trams. However, transfers cannot be made from the metro to a bus or tram.


  • Be fast getting on and off the metro because it only stops for a short period of time.
  • Prepare for the metro to be crowded.
  • Watch out for pickpockets. They usually use a bump and distract technique to steal valuables.
  • Use a metro map to plan routes.
  • Transfers can involve several minutes walking underground to the next transfer station. The routes are well marked.


RER

The RER is a local train that travels within Paris and to some nearby cities.



Reasons to Use the RER

  • The RER goes to Versailles, Charles de Gaulle Airport, and other suburban areas of Paris.
  • There is also an RER train that goes to Disneyland Paris.


Tips for Using the RER



  • A ticket for the RER includes the local metro and all transfers as long as visitors don't go above ground when transferring.
  • The RER doesn't run as frequently as the metro.

RER B can be used to go to and from the Charles de Gaulle Airport.
